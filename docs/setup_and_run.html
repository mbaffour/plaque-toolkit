5 · Your results (in out\)

FileWhat it is
data-green-<plate>.csvone per plate — a row per plaque (area & diameter in px and mm)
out_<plate>.<ext>the plate image with every plaque circled + numbered
summary.csvone row per plate: count, mean/median/min/max diameter, mean area

Per‑plaque columns: INDEX_COL, AREA_PXL, DIAMETER_PXL, AREA_MM2, DIAMETER_MM (+ MEAN_GRAY, TURBIDITY_REL — ignore these two for a pure‑original size result).

6 · Good to know

out\ accumulates. CSVs stay there until a same‑named plate overwrites them (or you delete them). The summary.csv reflects whatever is in out\ — so clear out old plates before a fresh set.
  • Dense / faint plates (like a near‑confluent lawn) are undercounted — treat the numbers as a size sample, not a titer. Keep plates in the 30–300 countable range.
  • Tilt warning: if it says the dish looks elliptical, re‑shoot straight‑on for accurate mm.
  • Original vs current: "Original (published)" reproduces the published Plaque Size Tool; the small differences vs current are ≤ ~0.04 mm with the count unchanged.
